Hearthlily — 晴萱

Premium Senior Lifestyle Concierge

An elegant, non-medical boutique care service built for independent elderly residents of Taipei, sold to their adult children who value trust, communication, and dignity.

📍 Location: Taipei City, Taiwan

⚙️ Tech Support: Morgan (Systems & Infra)

1. Executive Summary & Positioning

Hearthlily (晴萱) bridges the open middle ground in Taipei's elderly care market. Instead of competing with basic, high-churn hourly care agencies or heavy clinical live-in caregivers, Hearthlily offers high-trust companionship, digital safety coaching, medical accompaniment, and digital legacy organization.

The service is sold to the 35–55 "sandwich generation" of adult children (including overseas Taiwanese) who carry the cultural obligation of care (filial piety) but lack the daily time. They are not buying a basic chore broker; they are buying their parents' dignity and their own peace of mind.

"The premium concierge you would want for your own parents."

2. Market & Timing

  • A Super-Aged Reality: Taiwan officially became a super-aged society at the end of 2025 (over 20% of the population is 65+). Taipei City has the highest concentration in the country at 24.18%—nearly one in four residents.
  • Saturated Public Sector: The government's public system (LTC 3.0) is strictly means-tested, resource-constrained, and targets only severe physical or cognitive disabilities. Active, independent seniors are left unserved.
  • Félicia's Moat: As a Taiwanese native, Félicia overcomes the trust barrier. Her background in UI/UX and graphic design allows her to design a professional family reporting system (UX Family Report) and offer specialized digital legacy management.

4. Operations & Safety Standards

  • Premium Transport Policy: To maintain a premium image and guarantee safety, seniors are never transported on scooters. All travel during sessions utilizes pre-booked taxis or premium ride-hailing services. The costs are directly linked and charged to the client's card as a pass-through expense.
  • Zero Cash Handling Policy: Staff never handle cash, checkbooks, or bank credentials. All errands and reimbursements are processed through digital local rails (LINE Pay, ECPay, or NewebPay) to eliminate liability.
  • Three-Part Legal Onboarding: Every contract includes:
    1. A lawyer-drafted Non-Medical Co-Responsibility Agreement.
    2. An Emergency 119 Protocol Form to authorize immediate hospital transit.
    3. A Financial Boundary Policy document.
  • Strict Scope Boundary: Complete the local Care Attendant (照顧服務員) certification to build trust, but enforce a strict non-medical boundary (no injections or clinical care).

5. Two-Channel Marketing Strategy

Marketing targets native Taiwanese families through two distinct channels:

1. Acute-Need Channel (Volume)

Focuses on high-intent leads at the moment the family faces a care emergency:

  • Hospital discharge-planning units (出院準備服務).
  • Local neighborhood clinics and rehabilitation centers.
  • Pharmacies and medical supply outlets.

2. Wealth Channel (Positioning)

Builds brand authority and secures high-ticket projects:

  • Private wealth managers and family offices in Da'an/Xinyi.
  • Estate-planning and elder-law firms.
  • High-end clubs and community hubs.
